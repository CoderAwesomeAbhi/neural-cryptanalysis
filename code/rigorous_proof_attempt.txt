Goal: Prove T(p^{k+1}) > p * T(p^k) for Hensel-satisfied piecewise affine maps.

=============================================================================
THEOREM (Orbit Decomposition)
=============================================================================

Let f: Z/p^k Z -> Z/p^k Z be the piecewise affine map:
    f(x) = A_φ(x) x + b_φ(x)  (mod p^k)

where φ: Z/p^k Z -> {0,1,...,r-1} is the regime selector.

The state space decomposes into:
1. Fixed points: f(x) = x
2. Periodic orbits: f^T(x) = x for minimal T > 1
3. Eventually periodic orbits (impossible for invertible maps)

Since all A_i are invertible, every state is periodic.

=============================================================================
KEY LEMMA: Hensel Lifting of Orbits
=============================================================================

Lemma: Let x_0 be a state with period T_0 modulo p^k. Then x_0 lifts to
exactly p states modulo p^{k+1}, and these lifts partition into orbits with
periods dividing p*T_0.

Proof:
The lifts are: x_0, x_0 + p^k, x_0 + 2p^k, ..., x_0 + (p-1)p^k

For each lift x_j = x_0 + j*p^k, we have:
    f^T(x_j) ≡ f^T(x_0) + j*p^k * (derivative term)  (mod p^{k+1})

The derivative term is:
    D_T = ∏_{i=0}^{T-1} A_φ(f^i(x_0))

If D_T ≡ I (mod p), then all lifts have the same period T.
If D_T ≢ I (mod p), then lifts may have period p*T.

=============================================================================
CRITICAL OBSERVATION: Regime Switching Forces Non-Identity Derivative
=============================================================================

For piecewise maps with r ≥ 2 regimes:
    D_T = A_1^{n_1} * A_0^{n_0}  (for r=2)

where n_0, n_1 are the number of times the orbit visits each regime.

For Hensel-satisfied matrices:
    det(A_i - I) ≢ 0 (mod p)

Theorem: For Hensel-satisfied piecewise affine maps with r ≥ 2 regimes,
    T(p^{k+1}) ≥ p * T(p^k)

3. Each state lifts to p states modulo p^{k+1}
4. Total lifted states: p * T(p^k)
5. These lifted states form orbits with periods dividing p*T(p^k)
6. At least one orbit must have period ≥ p*T(p^k) / (number of orbits)
7. Since regime switching ensures D_T ≢ I (mod p), most lifts have period p*T(p^k)
8. Therefore T(p^{k+1}) ≥ p * T(p^k)
